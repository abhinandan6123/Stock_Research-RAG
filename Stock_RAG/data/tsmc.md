# TSMC (TSM)

TSMC is the world's largest dedicated semiconductor foundry, controlling ~90% of advanced node chip production below 5nm.

## AI Supply Chain Role
- Manufactures NVIDIA H100/H200/Blackwell, Apple A-series, AMD MI300X
- CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) packaging is a critical bottleneck for HBM+GPU stacking
- N3 (3nm) node in full production; N2 (2nm) entering risk production in 2025

## Capacity Bottlenecks
- CoWoS packaging capacity constrained through 2025
- 3nm demand from Apple, NVIDIA, AMD exceeds current supply
- Advanced packaging (SoIC, CoWoS) requires separate fab investment

## Geopolitical Risk
- ~90% of advanced node capacity located in Taiwan
- US-China tension creates supply chain concentration risk
- TSMC Arizona fab (N4 process) targeting production in 2025 as hedge

## Financial Highlights (2024)
- Revenue: ~$90B (estimate)
- ~60% of revenue from AI-related chips
- Gross Margin: ~55%
- Forward P/E: ~22x

## Strategic Importance
- No alternative to TSMC exists for sub-3nm chips
- Intel Foundry Services and Samsung are 2–3 years behind on yield
- Any disruption to TSMC = global AI hardware supply chain halt
