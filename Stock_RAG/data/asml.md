# ASML (ASML)

ASML holds a global monopoly on EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet) lithography machines required for advanced semiconductor fabrication.

## Monopoly Position
- Only company in the world that manufactures EUV lithography machines
- Every advanced chip (below 7nm) requires ASML EUV machines
- TSMC, Samsung, Intel all exclusively dependent on ASML

## EUV Machine Economics
- One EUV machine costs ~$200M
- 18-month lead time to manufacture and deliver
- TSMC ordered 100+ machines for 2025–2026 capacity expansion
- High NA EUV (next generation): $350M per machine, needed for 2nm and below

## AI Supply Chain Leverage
- AI chip demand → TSMC needs more capacity → TSMC orders more ASML machines
- ASML is the upstream chokepoint of the entire semiconductor supply chain
- Cannot be replicated: took 30+ years and $10B+ R&D to develop EUV technology

## Geopolitical Dimension
- Dutch government (under US pressure) blocks EUV exports to China
- Chinese fabs (SMIC) limited to older DUV technology; cannot make sub-7nm chips
- Export control is a structural competitive advantage for Western chip ecosystem

## Financial Highlights (2024)
- Revenue: ~€28B
- Gross Margin: ~51%
- Order Backlog: €39B+
- Forward P/E: ~32x

## Long-Term View
- Every new fab built globally requires ASML machines
- Upgrade cycle: existing EUV → High NA EUV creates recurring revenue
- No competitive threat visible within 10–15 year horizon
