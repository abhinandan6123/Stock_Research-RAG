# Nvidia (NVDA)

Nvidia dominates AI GPU production with its H100 and H200 accelerators.

## Supply Bottlenecks
- HBM3/HBM3e memory availability from SK Hynix and Samsung
- CoWoS advanced packaging capacity at TSMC
- Data center power and cooling infrastructure constraints
- Lead times for H100 GPUs stretched to 8–12 months in 2024

## Competitive Moat
- CUDA ecosystem: 4M+ developers, 3,500+ GPU-accelerated applications
- Switching cost from CUDA to AMD ROCm or Intel OneAPI is extremely high
- PyTorch and TensorFlow default to CUDA

## Financial Highlights (FY2024)
- Revenue: $60.9B (+122% YoY)
- Gross Margin: 74.6%
- Data Center segment: ~78% of total revenue

## Blackwell Architecture (2025)
- B100/B200 GPUs offer 2.5x performance improvement over H100
- GB200 NVL72 rack: 72 GPUs in NVLink domain, 1.4 exaFLOPS
- Volume production ramp expected Q2–Q3 2025

## Risks
- Top 4 hyperscaler customers represent ~46% of revenue
- China revenue dropped from 20% to under 5% due to export controls
- Custom silicon from Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium), Microsoft (Maia)

## Price Targets
- Bull: $1,500 | Base: $1,100 | Bear: $650
