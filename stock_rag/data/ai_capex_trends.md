# AI Capex Supercycle – 2024–2026 Trends

The four major hyperscalers committed over $200B in AI infrastructure spending for 2024–2025, representing an unprecedented technology investment cycle.

## Hyperscaler Commitments
- Microsoft: $80B (FY2025) — Azure AI data centers globally
- Google: $75B (2025) — TPU clusters + NVIDIA GPU farms
- Amazon: $75B (2025) — AWS data centers + Trainium custom chips
- Meta: $60–65B (2025) — LLaMA training clusters, AI recommendation systems

## What Is Driving the Spending
- Generative AI model training requires massive GPU clusters (10,000–100,000 GPUs)
- Inference demand growing faster than training as models deploy to users
- Sovereign AI: governments building national AI infrastructure
- Enterprise AI: Copilot, agents, automation driving SaaS add-on revenue

## Beneficiary Layers

### Infrastructure (Picks & Shovels)
- GPUs: Nvidia, AMD
- Networking: Arista Networks (ANET), Broadcom (AVGO)
- Power: Vertiv (VRT), Eaton (ETN), GE Vernova (GEV)
- Cooling: Modine Manufacturing, Comfort Systems USA

### Cloud Platforms
- Azure (Microsoft): OpenAI partnership, Copilot monetization
- AWS (Amazon): Bedrock, SageMaker, Trainium
- Google Cloud: Gemini, Vertex AI, TPU access

### AI Applications
- Salesforce (CRM): Agentforce, Einstein AI
- ServiceNow (NOW): AI workflow automation
- Palantir (PLTR): AIP for enterprise and government

## Training vs Inference Shift
- 2023–2024: Training dominated GPU demand
- 2025 onwards: Inference expected to be 60–70% of workload
- Inference favors: smaller optimized models, edge deployment
- New winners: ARM Holdings, Qualcomm for edge inference

## Key Risk: ROI Question
- Enterprise AI adoption slower than hyperscaler build-out implies
- If Copilot, AI agents don't drive measurable productivity → capex pullback
- Historical analog: Dot-com capex overshoot of 1999–2001
- Counter-argument: Real revenue already being generated (GitHub Copilot $2B ARR)
