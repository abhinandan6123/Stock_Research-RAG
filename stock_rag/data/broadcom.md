# Broadcom (AVGO)

Broadcom is the leading custom ASIC designer for AI accelerators, serving hyperscalers who want to reduce NVIDIA dependency.

## Custom ASIC Business
- Designs XPUs (custom AI chips) for Google (TPU v5), Meta, ByteDance
- AI revenue: $12B+ (FY2024)
- TAM guidance: $60–90B by FY2027 from 3 hyperscaler customers alone

## Why Hyperscalers Use Broadcom
- Custom chips optimized for specific workloads (e.g., Google Search, recommendation models)
- Lower cost per inference vs general-purpose NVIDIA GPUs
- No supply dependency on NVIDIA; own supply chain control

## Networking Business
- Tomahawk and Jericho switching silicon for AI data center fabric
- AI clusters require ultra-low-latency networking; Broadcom dominates
- Competes with Arista Networks at the software layer

## VMware Integration
- $69B acquisition of VMware closed November 2023
- Adds enterprise software recurring revenue: VCF (VMware Cloud Foundation)
- Cross-sell opportunity: VMware customers → Broadcom networking → AI infrastructure

## Financial Highlights
- Revenue: ~$52B (FY2024, including VMware)
- Gross Margin: ~68%
- Forward P/E: ~28x
- Free Cash Flow Margin: ~50%

## Risk
- Customer concentration: 3 hyperscalers = majority of AI ASIC revenue
- If hyperscalers slow AI capex → AVGO AI revenue directly impacted
- NVIDIA custom silicon efforts (Grace Blackwell) could compete in future
